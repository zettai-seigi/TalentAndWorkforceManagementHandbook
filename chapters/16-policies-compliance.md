# Chapter 16: Policies, Standards, and Compliance

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Develop comprehensive workforce policies that establish organizational requirements
- Create standards that define quality expectations for talent management
- Ensure compliance with employment laws including EEOC, ADA, FMLA, and GDPR
- Manage labor relations and collective bargaining requirements
- Implement contractor and contingent workforce policies
- Maintain audit readiness through proper documentation
- Design policy exception processes and enforcement mechanisms

---

## The Role of Policies and Standards

Policies and standards establish the rules, expectations, and requirements for workforce management. While governance provides structure and oversight, policies define what must be done, standards define how well it must be done, and compliance requirements establish the legal boundaries within which workforce activities must operate.

**Policies**: Mandatory requirements that all employees and managers must follow. Policies establish minimum expectations and enable accountability.

**Standards**: Quality expectations that define good practice and guide practitioners toward effective approaches. Standards are often more detailed than policies.

**Procedures**: Step-by-step instructions for executing specific workforce processes. Procedures operationalize policies and standards.

**Guidelines**: Recommended practices that are not mandatory. Guidelines provide helpful direction without creating requirements.

**Compliance Requirements**: Legal and regulatory requirements that organizations must meet to operate lawfully.

### Policy Components

Well-structured policies include:

**Purpose**: Why the policy exists and what it aims to achieve

**Scope**: Who and what the policy applies to (employees, contractors, locations)

**Policy Statements**: Specific mandatory requirements written clearly and concisely

**Definitions**: Key terms defined to ensure common understanding

**Roles and Responsibilities**: Who is responsible for compliance and enforcement

**Procedures**: How requirements are to be implemented (may be separate document)

**Exceptions**: How exceptions are requested, approved, and documented

**Consequences**: What happens in case of non-compliance

**References**: Related policies, laws, regulations

**Effective Date**: When policy takes effect

**Review Cycle**: When the policy will be reviewed and updated

## Key Workforce Policies

### Policy 1: Recruitment and Selection Policy

**Purpose**: To ensure fair, consistent, and legally compliant hiring practices that attract and select qualified candidates.

**Scope**: All regular employees, contractors, and temporary workers hired by the organization.

**Policy Statements**:

1. **Non-Discrimination**: All hiring decisions will be made without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or other protected characteristics.

2. **Position Authorization**: All positions must be approved and funded before recruitment begins.

3. **Job Descriptions**: Current, accurate job descriptions must exist for all positions before posting.

4. **Posting Requirements**: All positions must be posted internally for minimum 5 business days before external posting (except executive roles).

5. **Interview Process**: All candidates for a position must be evaluated using consistent criteria and structured interview questions.

6. **Background Checks**: Background checks must be completed for all new hires per the Background Check Policy.

7. **Immigration Compliance**: I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification must be completed within 3 days of hire.

8. **Documentation**: All hiring decisions must be documented with rationale and maintained per retention requirements.

**Responsibilities**:
- Hiring Managers: Create job requisitions, participate in interviews, make selection recommendations
- Talent Acquisition: Post positions, source candidates, coordinate interviews, conduct background checks
- HR Business Partners: Review requisitions, advise on hiring decisions, ensure compliance
- Legal: Review offer letters, advise on compliance matters

**Exceptions**: Exceptions require written approval from CHRO.

### Policy 2: Training and Development Policy

**Purpose**: To ensure employees have the knowledge, skills, and abilities required for their roles and to support career development.

**Scope**: All employees.

**Policy Statements**:

1. **Mandatory Training**: All employees must complete required compliance training within specified timeframes.

2. **Role-Based Training**: New employees must complete role-specific training during onboarding period.

3. **Manager Training**: All people managers must complete management fundamentals training within 90 days of assuming management responsibilities.

4. **Professional Development**: Employees may request professional development opportunities aligned with career goals and business needs.

5. **Tuition Assistance**: Eligible employees may receive tuition assistance for approved degree programs per the Tuition Assistance Policy.

6. **Training Records**: All training completion must be documented in the learning management system.

7. **Time for Training**: Employees must be provided work time to complete required training.

**Required Training by Category**:

| Employee Category | Required Training | Timeframe |
|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| All employees | Code of Conduct | Within 30 days of hire, annually thereafter |
| All employees | Information Security Basics | Within 30 days of hire, annually thereafter |
| All employees | Workplace Harassment Prevention | Within 30 days of hire, biennially thereafter |
| People managers | Management Fundamentals | Within 90 days of assuming role |
| People managers | Performance Management | Annually before review cycle |
| HR professionals | Employment Law Update | Annually |
| Finance staff | SOX Compliance Training | Annually |
| Technical staff | Role-specific certifications | Per certification requirements |

**Responsibilities**:
- Employees: Complete required training, pursue development opportunities
- Managers: Ensure direct reports complete required training, support development
- Learning & Development: Provide training programs, track completion, report compliance
- HR: Enforce training requirements, approve development requests

### Policy 3: Performance Management Policy

**Purpose**: To ensure consistent, fair, and effective performance management that drives individual and organizational success.

**Scope**: All employees except those in introductory period.

**Policy Statements**:

1. **Goal Setting**: All employees must have documented performance goals aligned with organizational and departmental objectives.

2. **Goal Documentation**: Goals must be documented in the performance management system within 30 days of plan year start or employee hire.

3. **Performance Reviews**: All employees must receive an annual performance review.

4. **Review Timing**: Annual reviews must be completed according to the performance management calendar.

5. **Performance Ratings**: Performance must be rated using the standard 5-point rating scale.

6. **Calibration**: Performance ratings must be calibrated within departments to ensure consistency.

7. **Performance Discussions**: Managers must discuss performance with employees at least quarterly.

8. **Documentation**: Performance discussions and issues must be documented contemporaneously.

9. **Performance Improvement**: Underperformance must be addressed through performance improvement plans.

10. **Link to Compensation**: Performance ratings inform merit increase and bonus decisions per the Compensation Policy.

**Performance Rating Scale**:

| Rating | Label | Definition | Expected Distribution |
|--------|-------|------------|----------------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds expectations in all areas; demonstrates extraordinary impact | 5-10% |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Frequently exceeds expectations; delivers strong results | 20-25% |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets all expectations; delivers solid performance | 50-60% |
| 2 | Partially Meets Expectations | Meets some but not all expectations; improvement needed | 10-15% |
| 1 | Does Not Meet Expectations | Fails to meet basic expectations; significant improvement required | 0-5% |

**Responsibilities**:
- Employees: Set goals with manager, seek feedback, meet performance expectations
- Managers: Set clear expectations, provide feedback, conduct reviews, address performance issues
- HR: Facilitate performance management process, calibrate ratings, advise on performance issues
- Senior Leadership: Ensure process compliance, review calibration, address systemic issues

### Policy 4: Compensation Policy

**Purpose**: To ensure fair, competitive, and internally equitable compensation practices that attract, retain, and motivate employees.

**Scope**: All employees.

**Policy Statements**:

1. **Compensation Philosophy**: Compensation will be competitive with the market median for comparable roles in our industry and geography.

2. **Salary Structure**: All positions will be assigned to a pay grade with defined salary ranges.

3. **Pay Positioning**: New hire salaries will generally be positioned between minimum and midpoint of the salary range based on qualifications and experience.

4. **Merit Increases**: Annual merit increases will be based on performance and position in salary range.

5. **Promotional Increases**: Promotions will result in salary increases of minimum 8-12% or to the minimum of the new grade, whichever is greater.

6. **Pay Equity**: Compensation decisions will ensure pay equity across gender, race, and other protected categories.

7. **Market Adjustments**: Periodic market adjustments may be made to address competitive concerns.

8. **Bonus Eligibility**: Bonus eligibility and targets are defined by job level per the Variable Compensation Plan.

9. **Salary Confidentiality**: Employees have the right to discuss their compensation; however, accessing others' compensation without authorization is prohibited.

**Salary Administration Guidelines**:

| Action | Guideline | Approval Required |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| New hire offer | Minimum to midpoint of range | Hiring manager, HR |
| New hire offer | Above midpoint | BU leader, CHRO |
| Merit increase | 0-5% based on performance | Manager, HR |
| Promotional increase | 8-12% or to new grade minimum | BU leader, HR, Compensation |
| Market adjustment | Based on market data | Compensation, CHRO |
| Retention adjustment | Based on retention risk | BU leader, CHRO |
| Out-of-cycle increase | Approved exceptions only | CHRO |

**Responsibilities**:
- Compensation: Maintain salary structures, conduct market analyses, ensure pay equity
- HR Business Partners: Advise managers on compensation decisions, review for equity
- Managers: Make compensation recommendations within guidelines
- Finance: Ensure compensation decisions align with budget

### Policy 5: Termination and Separation Policy

**Purpose**: To ensure consistent, legally compliant, and respectful handling of employment terminations and separations.

**Scope**: All employees.

**Policy Statements**:

1. **Employment At-Will**: Employment is at-will and may be terminated by either party at any time, with or without cause or notice (except where prohibited by law or contract).

2. **Involuntary Termination**: Involuntary terminations must be approved by HR and reviewed by Legal before execution.

3. **Performance-Based Termination**: Performance-based terminations must be supported by documented performance issues and performance improvement plan (except in introductory period).

4. **Conduct-Based Termination**: Conduct-based terminations must be supported by investigation findings and documented policy violations.

5. **Layoffs and Reductions**: Workforce reductions must comply with WARN Act requirements and be approved by executive leadership.

6. **Notice Period**: Organization may provide notice period or pay in lieu of notice at its discretion.

7. **Final Paycheck**: Final paycheck including accrued vacation will be provided per state law requirements.

8. **Benefits Continuation**: COBRA notification and continuation coverage information will be provided per legal requirements.

9. **Exit Interview**: Exit interviews should be conducted with all departing employees.

10. **Company Property**: All company property must be returned on or before last day of employment.

11. **System Access**: System access will be terminated on last day of employment or immediately upon termination for cause.

**Termination Approval Matrix**:

| Termination Type | Documentation Required | Approval Required |
|------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Resignation | Resignation letter | Manager acknowledgment |
| Performance (after PIP) | Performance documentation, completed PIP | Manager, HR Director, Legal review |
| Performance (introductory period) | Performance documentation | Manager, HR BP |
| Misconduct | Investigation report, policy violation evidence | Manager, HR Director, Legal review |
| Layoff/RIF | Business justification, selection criteria | BU leader, CHRO, CEO |
| Executive | Varies | CHRO, CEO, Board (as appropriate) |

**Responsibilities**:
- Managers: Document performance/conduct issues, recommend termination with justification
- HR: Review termination decisions, ensure compliance, coordinate termination process
- Legal: Review terminations for legal risk, advise on approach, review separation agreements
- IT: Terminate system access
- Facilities: Recover company property

### Policy 6: Contractor and Contingent Workforce Policy

**Purpose**: To ensure appropriate use, management, and compliance for contractor and contingent workforce engagements.

**Scope**: All contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and other contingent workers engaged by the organization.

**Policy Statements**:

1. **Approval Required**: All contractor engagements must be approved by hiring manager, HR, and Procurement before engagement.

2. **Classification**: Worker classification (employee vs. contractor) must be determined using IRS and DOL guidelines before engagement.

3. **Misclassification Prevention**: Workers who should be classified as employees must not be engaged as contractors.

4. **Statement of Work**: All contractor engagements must have a signed statement of work defining scope, deliverables, rate, and duration.

5. **Rate Card Compliance**: Contractor rates must align with approved rate cards or require exception approval.

6. **Background Checks**: Background checks must be completed for contractors with access to sensitive data or facilities.

7. **Onboarding**: Contractors must complete compliance training and system access provisioning.

8. **Duration Limits**: Contractor engagements exceeding 12 consecutive months require CHRO approval and review for co-employment risk.

9. **System Access**: Contractor system access must be appropriately limited and terminated at end of engagement.

10. **Supervision**: Contractors must not supervise employees or make employment decisions.

11. **Benefits Ineligibility**: Contractors are not eligible for employee benefits.

**Contractor Engagement Process**:

        |

        |

        |

Onboarding and System Access (IT + HR)
        |

**Responsibilities**:
- Managers: Justify contractor need, supervise contractor work
- HR: Assess worker classification, approve engagements, monitor duration
- Procurement: Source contractors, negotiate terms, manage vendor relationships
- Legal: Review classification decisions, advise on co-employment risk

## Employment Law Compliance

### Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) Compliance

**Legal Foundation**:
- Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964
- Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA)
- Equal Pay Act

**Compliance Requirements**:

1. **Non-Discrimination**: Employment decisions must be based on job-related criteria without regard to protected characteristics.

2. **EEO-1 Reporting**: Organizations with 100+ employees must file annual EEO-1 reports with EEOC.

3. **Applicant Data Collection**: Applicant demographic data must be collected separately from application materials.

4. **Job Descriptions**: Job descriptions must specify essential functions and requirements that are job-related and consistent with business necessity.

5. **Interview Questions**: Interview questions must be job-related and asked consistently across candidates.

6. **Adverse Impact Analysis**: Selection procedures must be analyzed for adverse impact on protected groups.

7. **Recordkeeping**: Employment records must be retained per EEOC requirements (generally 1 year for applications, 2 years for personnel files).

**Protected Characteristics**:

| Protected Class | Relevant Laws | Key Prohibitions |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------|
| Race/Color | Title VII | Discrimination, segregation, harassment |
| National Origin | Title VII | Discrimination, language requirements without business necessity |
| Religion | Title VII | Discrimination, failure to accommodate sincerely held beliefs |
| Sex/Gender | Title VII, Equal Pay Act | Discrimination, pay inequity, pregnancy discrimination |
| Sexual Orientation | Title VII (per SCOTUS) | Discrimination, harassment |
| Gender Identity | Title VII (per SCOTUS) | Discrimination, harassment |
| Age (40+) | ADEA | Discrimination, mandatory retirement (with exceptions) |
| Disability | ADA | Discrimination, failure to provide reasonable accommodation |
| Genetic Information | GINA | Discrimination, requesting genetic information |

### Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Compliance

**Compliance Requirements**:

1. **Reasonable Accommodation**: Employers must provide reasonable accommodations for qualified individuals with disabilities unless it creates undue hardship.

2. **Interactive Process**: Engage in interactive dialogue with employees requesting accommodations to identify effective accommodations.

3. **Medical Inquiries**: Limit medical inquiries to job-related questions about ability to perform essential functions.

4. **Pre-Employment**: Cannot ask about disabilities before offering employment; can ask about ability to perform job functions.

5. **Confidentiality**: Medical information must be kept confidential in separate medical files.

6. **Accessible Facilities**: Ensure physical facilities are accessible to individuals with disabilities.

**Accommodation Process**:

```
Employee Requests Accommodation
        |
        v
HR Acknowledges Request (within 24-48 hours)
        |
        v
Interactive Process Initiated
        |
        v
Medical Documentation Requested (if needed)
        |
        v
Medical Documentation Reviewed
        |
        v
Accommodation Options Identified
        |
        v
Feasibility and Undue Hardship Analysis
        |
        v
Accommodation Decision Made
        |
        v
Accommodation Implemented or Alternative Discussed
        |
        v
Effectiveness Monitored
        |
        v
Periodic Review and Adjustment
```

**Common Accommodations**:
- Modified work schedule or flexible hours
- Remote work arrangements
- Assistive technology or equipment
- Workspace modifications
- Reassignment to vacant position
- Modified duties (temporary)
- Extended leave (beyond FMLA)

### Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) Compliance

**Eligibility Requirements**:
- Employee must work for covered employer (50+ employees within 75 miles)
- Employee must have worked 1,250 hours in preceding 12 months
- Employee must have worked for employer for 12 months

**Qualifying Reasons**:
- Birth and care of newborn
- Adoption or foster care placement
- Care for spouse, child, or parent with serious health condition
- Employee's own serious health condition
- Military family leave (qualifying exigency or military caregiver)

**Leave Entitlement**:
- Up to 12 weeks in 12-month period for most reasons
- Up to 26 weeks in 12-month period for military caregiver leave

**Compliance Requirements**:

1. **Posting**: FMLA poster must be displayed in workplace.

2. **Notice to Employees**: Eligibility notice must be provided within 5 business days of leave request.

3. **Medical Certification**: Employer may require medical certification for serious health condition leave.

4. **Designation Notice**: Designation notice must be provided within 5 business days of having sufficient information.

5. **Job Protection**: Employee must be restored to same or equivalent position upon return.

6. **Benefits Continuation**: Health insurance must be maintained during FMLA leave.

7. **Recordkeeping**: FMLA records must be retained for 3 years.

**FMLA Administration Process**:

| Step | Timeframe | Responsible Party | Action |
|------|-----------|------------------|--------|
| Employee requests leave | As soon as practicable | Employee | Notify manager and HR |
| Eligibility notice | Within 5 business days | HR | Provide eligibility and rights notice |
| Medical certification request | Within 5 business days | HR | Request certification form |
| Medical certification submitted | Within 15 calendar days | Employee | Submit completed certification |
| Designation notice | Within 5 business days of adequate information | HR | Designate leave as FMLA-qualifying |
| Leave taken | Per approved schedule | Employee | Take leave |
| Benefits maintained | Throughout leave | HR/Benefits | Continue health insurance |
| Return to work | End of leave | Employee | Return to same/equivalent position |
| Records retained | 3 years | HR | Maintain FMLA documentation |

### General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Compliance

**Applicability**: Organizations processing personal data of EU residents.

**Employee Data Rights Under GDPR**:

1. **Right to Access**: Employees can request access to their personal data.

2. **Right to Rectification**: Employees can request correction of inaccurate data.

3. **Right to Erasure**: Employees can request deletion of data (with limitations).

4. **Right to Data Portability**: Employees can request data in machine-readable format.

5. **Right to Object**: Employees can object to certain data processing activities.

6. **Right to Restriction**: Employees can request restriction of data processing.

**Compliance Requirements for Employee Data**:

1. **Lawful Basis**: Establish lawful basis for processing employee data (typically employment contract or legal obligation).

2. **Data Minimization**: Collect only data necessary for legitimate purposes.

3. **Purpose Limitation**: Use data only for specified, legitimate purposes.

4. **Consent**: Obtain explicit consent for processing not covered by employment contract (e.g., photos for website).

5. **Data Security**: Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures.

6. **Breach Notification**: Notify supervisory authority within 72 hours of data breach (if high risk to individuals).

7. **Data Protection Impact Assessment**: Conduct DPIA for high-risk processing activities.

8. **Data Retention**: Retain data only as long as necessary; delete when no longer needed.

9. **Third-Party Processors**: Ensure data processing agreements with vendors.

**Employee Data Categories and Retention**:

| Data Category | Lawful Basis | Retention Period | Storage Location |
|---------------|--------------|------------------|------------------|
| Contact information | Employment contract | 7 years post-termination | HRIS |
| Job applications | Legitimate interest | 2 years post-application | Applicant tracking system |
| Performance reviews | Employment contract | 7 years post-termination | Performance management system |
| Payroll data | Legal obligation | 7+ years (per local law) | Payroll system |
| Medical information | Legal obligation, consent | Per local requirements | Secure medical files |
| Disciplinary records | Legitimate interest | 7 years post-termination | HR case management system |
| Training records | Employment contract | Duration + 7 years | Learning management system |

## Labor Relations and Collective Bargaining

### Union-Free Organizations

**Preventive Labor Relations**:
- Maintain competitive compensation and benefits
- Provide fair, consistent treatment of employees
- Ensure effective communication and grievance procedures
- Train managers on prohibited conduct during organizing attempts
- Monitor employee relations climate and address issues proactively

**Manager Training Topics**:
- Employee rights under National Labor Relations Act
- Protected concerted activity
- Prohibited conduct (TIPS: Threaten, Interrogate, Promise, Spy)
- How to respond to union organizing attempts
- Reporting requirements

### Unionized Organizations

**Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) Compliance**:

1. **CBA Administration**: Ensure all terms of collective bargaining agreement are followed.

2. **Management Rights**: Exercise management rights consistent with CBA provisions.

3. **Grievance Procedures**: Follow grievance procedures specified in CBA.

4. **Union Notification**: Provide required notice to union for changes affecting bargaining unit employees.

5. **Duty to Bargain**: Negotiate in good faith regarding mandatory subjects of bargaining.

6. **Weingarten Rights**: Provide union representation when requested during investigatory interviews.

7. **Information Requests**: Respond to union requests for relevant information.

**Subjects of Bargaining**:

| Category | Examples | Bargaining Required |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| Mandatory | Wages, hours, working conditions, benefits, discipline | Yes |
| Permissive | Union security clauses, supervisory issues | Only if both parties agree |
| Illegal | Closed shop, discrimination | No - prohibited by law |

**Contract Administration**:
- Management training on CBA terms and restrictions
- Consistent application of CBA provisions
- Documentation of disciplinary actions per CBA procedures
- Coordination with union on changes affecting bargaining unit
- Preparation for contract negotiations and renewal

## Policy Exception Management

### Exception Process

Not all situations fit standard policies. A clear exception process enables appropriate flexibility:

**1. Exception Request**
- Exception requested in writing by manager or employee
- Justification provided explaining need for exception
- Alternative approaches considered
- Impact assessment on employee, team, organization

**2. Risk Assessment**
- HR reviews request for compliance risk
- Legal reviews for legal risk (if appropriate)
- Precedent implications considered
- Equity implications assessed

**3. Decision**
- Exception approved, denied, or modified
- Decision authority per exception matrix
- Conditions or limitations specified if approved
- Time limitation specified if temporary exception

**4. Documentation**
- Exception request and justification documented
- Decision and rationale documented
- Conditions and limitations documented
- Review date scheduled (if ongoing exception)

**5. Monitoring**
- Exception logged in policy exception tracker
- Periodic review of ongoing exceptions
- Analysis of exception patterns to identify policy improvement opportunities

### Exception Authority Matrix

| Policy Area | Exception Authority | Escalation Path |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Recruitment process deviation | Talent Acquisition Lead | HR Director → CHRO |
| Training requirement waiver | Learning & Development Lead | HR Director → CHRO |
| Performance management timing | HR Business Partner | HR Director |
| Compensation guideline exception | Compensation Director | CHRO → CEO |
| Leave policy exception | HR Director | CHRO |
| Termination process deviation | HR Director + Legal | CHRO + General Counsel |
| Contractor engagement exception | HR Director + Procurement | CHRO |

### Policy Review and Updates

**Review Triggers**:
- Scheduled review cycle (annually or biannually)
- Regulatory changes affecting policy area
- Audit findings requiring policy updates
- Exception patterns indicating policy inadequacy
- Organizational changes affecting policy applicability
- Feedback from managers or employees

**Review Process**:
1. Policy owner reviews current policy
2. Compliance with current law verified
3. Alignment with current practices assessed
4. Feedback from stakeholders solicited
5. Updates drafted if needed
6. Review and approval process executed
7. Updated policy communicated and implemented

## Summary

Policies, standards, and compliance requirements provide the framework for legal, ethical, and effective workforce management. Comprehensive policies establish mandatory requirements across recruitment, training, performance management, compensation, and termination. Employment law compliance requires adherence to multiple federal and state laws including EEO, ADA, FMLA, and GDPR for organizations with EU employees.

Labor relations management differs between union-free and unionized organizations, with collective bargaining agreement administration requiring careful attention to contractual obligations and union notification requirements. Contractor and contingent workforce policies prevent misclassification and co-employment risks while enabling appropriate workforce flexibility.

Audit readiness depends on comprehensive documentation of workforce decisions and transactions, with particular focus on high-risk areas such as FLSA compliance, I-9 administration, and FMLA management. Policy exception processes enable appropriate flexibility while maintaining equity and legal compliance.

Effective policies balance clarity with practicality—specific enough to ensure compliance and consistency, flexible enough to accommodate legitimate business needs, and practical enough to be followed by managers and employees. Regular policy review and updates ensure policies remain aligned with legal requirements, business needs, and organizational values.
