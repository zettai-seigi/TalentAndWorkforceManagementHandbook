### Visual 16.2: Policy vs. Procedure vs. Standard vs. Guideline

**Type**: Comparison Matrix / Infographic

**Title**: Understanding Policy Documentation Types: Requirements and Applications

**Chapter Reference**: Chapter 16, Section "Policy vs. Procedure vs. Guideline"

**Purpose**: Clarify distinctions between different types of workforce documentation to ensure appropriate creation and application.

**Content Description**:
- **Four-Quadrant Layout**: One quadrant per document type
- **Quadrant 1 - Policies** (Top Left, Red theme):
  - Definition: "What MUST be done"
  - Characteristics: Mandatory, No discretion, Violations = discipline
  - Icon: Stop sign or gavel
  - Example: "All employees must complete Code of Conduct training within 30 days of hire"
  - Approval: Senior leadership required
- **Quadrant 2 - Standards** (Top Right, Orange theme):
  - Definition: "How WELL to do it"
  - Characteristics: Quality expectations, Benchmarks, Measurable
  - Icon: Checkmark with ruler
  - Example: "Interview scorecards should be completed within 24 hours"
  - Approval: Department head
- **Quadrant 3 - Procedures** (Bottom Left, Blue theme):
  - Definition: "HOW to do it"
  - Characteristics: Step-by-step, Detailed instructions, Process-specific
  - Icon: Flowchart or numbered list
  - Example: "New hire training workflow: 1) HR creates account, 2) Manager assigns training..."
  - Approval: Process owner
- **Quadrant 4 - Guidelines** (Bottom Right, Green theme):
  - Definition: "RECOMMENDED approaches"
  - Characteristics: Suggested, Not mandatory, Adaptable
  - Icon: Lightbulb or compass
  - Example: "Consider scheduling performance conversations early in the day"
  - Approval: Subject matter expert
- **Center Circle**: "Documentation Framework" connecting all four types
- **Bottom Comparison Bar**: Flexibility scale showing Policy (rigid) to Guideline (flexible)

### Visual 16.5: Policy Exception and Escalation Framework

**Visual ID**: 16-Figure-16.5

**Type**: Flowchart with Decision Tree

**Title**: Policy Exception Request and Approval Process

**Chapter Reference**: Chapter 16, Section "Exceptions" (in policy components)

**Purpose**: Define clear process for requesting, evaluating, approving, and documenting policy exceptions.

**Content Description**:
- **Start**: Employee/Manager identifies need for policy exception
- **Step 1**: Complete exception request form
  - Required information: Policy reference, Business justification, Duration, Impact assessment, Alternative approaches considered
- **Step 2**: HRBP initial review
  - Decision diamond: "Meets exception criteria?"
  - No → Return to requestor with guidance
  - Yes → Proceed to Step 3
- **Step 3**: Risk assessment
  - Compliance impact?
  - Legal review needed?
  - Precedent implications?
- **Step 4**: Approval routing based on risk level
  - Low Risk → Department Head approval
  - Medium Risk → HR Director + Legal review
  - High Risk → CHRO + General Counsel + Executive approval
- **Step 5**: Exception approval/denial decision
  - If approved: Document + Set expiration + Notify stakeholders
  - If denied: Communicate rationale + Propose alternatives
- **Step 6**: Exception monitoring
  - Track active exceptions
  - Report quarterly to Governance Committee
  - Review at expiration
- **Step 7**: Exception closure
  - Renew (rare)
  - Allow to expire
  - Incorporate into policy if pattern emerges
- **Side Panel**: Exception tracking dashboard
  - Active exceptions by policy area
  - Exception approval rate
  - Most frequently requested exceptions
- **Color Coding**: Green path for approval, Red for denial, Yellow for additional review needed

**Specifications**:
- **Dimensions**: 1400px × 1800px (portrait)
- **Colors**:
  - Process boxes: Primary Blue (#0066CC) outline, white fill
  - Decision diamonds: Accent Orange (#FF6B35) outline, Light Gray fill
  - Approval path: Success Green (#28A745) arrows
  - Denial path: Red (#DC3545) arrows
  - Review path: Warning Yellow (#FFC107) arrows
  - Start/End ovals: Dark Gray (#333333) fill, white text
- **Fonts**:
  - Title: 26pt, Bold
  - Process boxes: 14pt, Regular
  - Decision text: 14pt, Bold Italic
  - Notes/details: 11pt, Regular
- **Tools**: Microsoft Visio, Lucidchart, or Draw.io

**Caption**: The policy exception framework provides a consistent, risk-based process for evaluating and approving exceptions while maintaining governance oversight and preventing policy erosion.
