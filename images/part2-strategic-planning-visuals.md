### Visual 04-Figure-4.2: Strategic Workforce Segmentation Matrix

**Type**: 2x2 Matrix / Strategic Quadrant Analysis

**Title**: Strategic Workforce Segmentation: Prioritizing Investment by Value and Scarcity

**Chapter Reference**: Chapter 4, Section "Workforce Segmentation Strategies"

**Purpose**: Help organizations prioritize workforce investments by segmenting roles based on strategic value and labor market scarcity, with differentiated strategies for each quadrant.

**Content Description**:
- **Structure**: 2x2 matrix with four distinct quadrants
- **Vertical Axis**: Strategic Value to Organization (Low to High)
- **Horizontal Axis**: Labor Market Scarcity/Difficulty to Replace (Low to High)
- **Four Quadrants**:
  1. **Bottom-Left: "Transactional"** (Light Gray)
     - Examples: Basic data entry, Level 1 service desk (routine), Standard documentation
     - Characteristics: Low strategic value, readily available in market
     - Strategy:
       - Efficiency focus, cost optimization
       - Process automation priority
       - Consider outsourcing
       - Acceptable higher attrition (15-25%)
       - Minimal development investment
  2. **Top-Left: "Necessary Support"** (Primary Blue)
     - Examples: Service desk (skilled), Application support, Network operations, System administrators
     - Characteristics: High strategic value, available in market with effort
     - Strategy:
       - Balanced approach
       - Training pipelines and development programs
       - Competitive total rewards
       - Moderate attrition acceptable (10-15%)
       - Career pathing to retain talent
  3. **Bottom-Right: "Rare but Low Impact"** (Warning Yellow)
     - Examples: Legacy system specialists, Obsolete technology experts, Niche but declining skills
     - Characteristics: Rare skills but declining strategic importance
     - Strategy:
       - Minimize investment
       - Plan transition/reskilling
       - Short-term contracts for critical needs
       - Knowledge transfer priority
       - Phase out as technology evolves
  4. **Top-Right: "Strategic Core"** (Success Green)
     - Examples: Cloud architects, Security architects, DevOps experts, AI/ML engineers, Customer relationship experts, Proprietary system specialists
     - Characteristics: High strategic value AND scarce in market
     - Strategy:
       - Protect and retain at all costs
       - Heavy development investment
       - Premium compensation
       - Succession planning critical
       - Build internal pipelines
       - Very low attrition target (0-5%)
       - Academic/industry partnerships

### Visual 05-Figure-5.3: Full-Cycle Recruiting Process Flow

**Title**: End-to-End Talent Acquisition Process: Roles and Activities

**Chapter Reference**: Chapter 5, Section "Recruitment Strategy Development"

**Purpose**: Map the complete recruitment process from requisition approval through onboarding, showing activities, responsibilities, decision points, and typical timelines across stakeholder roles.

**Content Description**:
- **Structure**: Horizontal swim lane diagram with 5 stakeholder lanes and 8 process stages
- **Stakeholder Swim Lanes** (vertical lanes):
  1. Hiring Manager
  2. Recruiter/TA
  3. Interview Team
  4. HR/People Ops
  5. Candidate (shown as interaction points)
- **Process Stages** (horizontal flow):
  1. **Requisition & Planning** (1-2 weeks)
     - Hiring manager: Submit req, define requirements, create interview plan
     - Recruiter: Approve req, source strategy, role marketing
     - Decision point: Req approval
  2. **Sourcing & Attraction** (1-3 weeks)
     - Recruiter: Post jobs, active sourcing, talent community outreach
     - Candidate: Discover opportunity, express interest
  3. **Screening & Assessment** (1-2 weeks)
     - Recruiter: Resume screen, phone screen, schedule
     - Candidate: Apply, complete screening
     - Decision point: Move to interview
  4. **Interviewing** (1-3 weeks)
     - Hiring manager: Conduct interviews, evaluate
     - Interview team: Conduct interviews, provide feedback
     - Recruiter: Coordinate, collect feedback
     - Candidate: Participate in interviews
     - Decision point: Offer/no offer
  5. **Offer & Negotiation** (1-2 weeks)
     - Hiring manager: Approve offer
     - HR: Prepare offer letter
     - Recruiter: Extend offer, negotiate
     - Candidate: Accept/decline/negotiate
     - Decision point: Offer acceptance
  6. **Pre-boarding** (2-4 weeks)
     - HR: Background check, paperwork, IT setup
     - Recruiter: Stay engaged, answer questions
     - Candidate: Complete requirements, prepare
  7. **Onboarding** (1-3 months)
     - Hiring manager: Execute onboarding plan
     - HR: Orientation, systems setup
     - Candidate: Ramp up, integrate
  8. **Evaluation** (Day 90)
     - All: Assess success, feedback, lessons learned
