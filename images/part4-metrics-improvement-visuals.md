**Six-Step Process**:

---

**Step 1: Data Collection** (Primary Blue)

**Data Sources**:
1. **HRIS Data**:
   - Tenure, role, level, department
   - Compensation, promotion history
   - Performance ratings
   - Manager changes

2. **Activity Data**:
   - Training completion
   - Internal job applications
   - Recognition received

   - Survey responses

**Data Preparation**:
- Remove personally identifiable information (PII)

- Create feature variables
- Split into training (70%) and test (30%) sets

---

**Step 2: Feature Engineering** (Accent Orange)

**Feature Categories and Examples**:

| Category | Features | Why Predictive |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| **Tenure** | Months in role, company, with manager | High risk at 12-18 months and 3-5 years |
| **Compensation** | Compa-ratio, time since raise, relative to peers | Below-market compensation drives exits |
| **Performance** | Latest rating, trend, calibration changes | Both high and low performers at risk |
| **Engagement** | Survey scores, trend, change from prior | Declining engagement predicts turnover |
| **Career** | Months since promotion, promotion velocity | Stagnation increases flight risk |
| **Manager** | Manager tenure, team turnover rate | Manager quality impacts retention |
| **Activity** | Internal applications, training, recognition | Checking out behaviors |
| **Life Events** | Recent life changes (data permitting) | Relocation, family changes |

**Feature Engineering Examples**:
- "Months since last promotion": Current date - last promotion date
- "Pay percentile vs. peers": Rank(salary) within (role + level + location)
- "Engagement trend": Current score - prior survey score
- "Manager turnover rate": % of manager's team that left in last 12 months

**Total Features**: 45 variables engineered from raw data

**Step 3: Model Training** (Success Green)

**Algorithm Selection**:
